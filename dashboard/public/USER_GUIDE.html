WHY THIS APP EXISTS

El Niño brings below-normal rainfall to large parts of the Philippines, and the damage it does to rice and corn depends entirely on timing — a dry spell during a crop's early growth is a setback; the same dry spell during its reproductive stage (when it needs water most) can wipe out the harvest. The science behind that is solid — but it lives in seasonal outlooks, crop calendars, and station data that take real effort to connect. This app explores how AI can help make that usable: pulling those pieces together and turning them into something specific and plain-language — for your province, your crop, at its exact growth stage, this week.

It combines PAGASA's rainfall outlook with each crop's current growth-stage sensitivity to score every province × crop combination from Low to High risk, then uses AI to write a short, specific advisory in English and Filipino — plus an SMS-ready version cooperatives can forward directly to farmers. It's an experimental prototype — a way to prioritise what to check, not an official or validated risk figure (see section 8).

2Reading the risk summary

The top of the dashboard gives you the headline number before anything else.

  • Risk summary bar — how many province × crop assessments fall into High (score > 65), Medium (35–65), or Low (< 35) risk this week.
  • Week of (top right) — the date this batch of scores was computed. The pipeline runs every Monday morning.
🧮 Where the number comes from risk_score = rainfall severity × crop-stage sensitivity × 100. In plain terms: how dry PAGASA expects the season to be, multiplied by how vulnerable the crop is at its current growth stage. The same dry forecast scores very differently for a newly-planted field versus one about to flower. This is an experimental heuristic — the score is a relative priority, not a validated measure of actual crop loss (see section 8).

5Province cards & advisories

Each card is one province × crop combination. This is where the actual guidance lives.

  • Header — province, region, crop, season, trend arrow (↑ rising / ↓ falling / → stable / NEW), and the risk badge with its numeric score.
  • Quick facts — crop growth stage, PAGASA outlook label, rainfall anomaly %, and the week.
  • Show advisory ▼ — expands to reveal:
    • An 8-week risk trend sparkline, so you can see if things are getting better or worse.
    • A 3-sentence AI-generated advisory in English or Filipino — it follows the dashboard-wide language switch in the top-right corner.
    • A ready-to-send SMS text version, already trimmed to fit one message.
    • A Share this advisory button — opens your phone's share sheet (or copies the text on desktop) so you can forward the advisory, in your chosen language, straight to a group chat or SMS.

7How often this updates

A fully automated weekly cycle — nobody has to remember to run anything.

  • Every Monday at 6:00 AM (Philippine Time), the system automatically: fetches the latest PAGASA seasonal outlook → recomputes every province × crop risk score → generates fresh AI advisories → sends SMS alerts to registered cooperative contacts → this dashboard picks up the new numbers on next load.
  • If a week ever shows no data, it means that run hasn't happened yet — check back after Monday morning.

8What this is — and isn't

Read this before you use anything on the dashboard to make a decision.

⚠️ An experimental tool — not an official or scientifically-validated advisory Risk scores are indicative estimates from a heuristic model, not an official or peer-reviewed methodology. They help you decide what to check on first; they do not measure actual crop loss. Always verify with your local Department of Agriculture or PAGASA office before acting on a High-risk reading.
🔬 How the score is built — and what it is not It multiplies two things: how far below normal PAGASA expects rainfall to be, and how sensitive the crop is at its current growth stage (rice is most sensitive at flowering — that part is well-established science). But the exact sensitivity numbers, the scaling, and the Low/Medium/High cut-offs are indicative — they haven't been calibrated against real recorded harvest losses, and no official body has validated them. So read a score as a relative priority ("check this province before that one"), not as an exact or guaranteed measure of risk.

9Who this is for

Built for the people closest to the farmers, in the pilot area.

  • LGU agricultural officers — a weekly, province-level snapshot to inform local guidance and planning.
  • Cooperative officers — receive SMS advisories directly and can forward the ready-made text to farmer members.
  • DA field staff — a quick reference alongside official PAGASA bulletins when briefing communities.
🌱 Pilot scope Currently covers Luzon pilot provinces, palay and corn. All 82 Philippine provinces are seeded in the system ahead of the next expansion phase.

10On the roadmap

Planned improvements — not yet available, listed so you know where this is heading.

  • Nationwide coverage — all 82 provinces, not just the Luzon pilot.
  • More crops — beyond palay and corn (for example sugarcane and high-value vegetables).
  • More languages — advisories in Ilocano and Cebuano, alongside English and Filipino.
  • Two-way SMS — cooperatives can reply to an alert to confirm they acted, so the system can track real impact on the ground.
  • High-risk alerts — opt in to get an email or notification the moment a province you follow crosses into High risk, instead of checking manually.
  • Printable weekly advisory — one tap to a print-ready PDF for briefings and barangay bulletin boards.
💡 Have a request? This is a pilot, and the roadmap is shaped by the LGU and cooperative officers using it. If something here would help your work — or something's missing — that feedback is what decides what gets built next.
